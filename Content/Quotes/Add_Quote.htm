Creating a quote

A quote is the price for the job that you have received from a customer, it should contain material and the labour cost for the job. You can send several quotes for a job via email to your customers. In a quote you can also choose if a customer is required to pay a deposit amount and what the amount is. When a customer approves the quote or pays the deposit amount, the status of the quote and the job will change to accepted.

To add a quote to a job follow the steps given below,

  1. Create a new quote by either,

    • Selecting New Quote in the quotes section of a job. If you create a quote from within a job, the customer name and job description are pre-filled with the customer and job details that you entered for the job.

      This is the recommended method.

    • Selecting the New Quote option in the quotes list. If you created your quote using the New Quote option of the quote list, you will have to fill in the customer (if they are not an existing customer) and job details sections. For more information on adding customer and job details see Adding customer information.

      Creating a new quote from the quote list also creates a new job corresponding to the quote that you created.

    The New Quote screen is displayed for you to enter quote estimates.

  2. In the Items section of the quote select new item .

    This displays the Item screen.

  3. in the Name field, enter either a name or code for the item you want to add to the invoice.

    Each item that you add to a quote in this method is saved for reuse in other quotes and invoices.

  4. In the Item description field, enter a description for the item.

  5. Use the Quantity field to add the amount of items that you are quoting the customer for.

  6. Next, enter the Unit Price of the item.

    The Subtotal field is automatically calculated for you, using the values that you entered into the Quantity and Unit Price fields.

  7. If you want to change the Tax Rate that is applicable for the item select the field.

    You can choose to have no GST applied to the item by selecting None. By default, the tax rate is set at 10%.

    The GST amount for the item is automatically calculated for you and displayed according to the Tax rate and the Subtotal of the item.

  8. Select Add to add the item to the quote or select Cancel if you do not want to add the item to the quote.

    Each item that you add to the quote is then displayed in the Items section.

    The Subtotal and GST fields for the whole quote are automatically calculated and displayed according to the subtotal of each item in your quote.

  9. Use the Deposit section to add a deposit amount to your quote if needed, see Adding deposits to a quote for more information.

  10. If you want to open the associated job, select the job from the Associated Job section.

  11. Select Send to email the quote to your customer or select Save to save the quote as a draft.
