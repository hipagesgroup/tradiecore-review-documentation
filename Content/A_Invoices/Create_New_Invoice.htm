Creating a new invoice

Follow the instructions below to create an invoice: 

Creating an Invoice

  1. Select Jobs on the navigation bar, then do one of the following:

    • Open a job and select the Create Invoice option.

      Note:

      Creating an invoice from within a job automatically fills in the customer and job details, keeping everything linked and easy to track.

    • Select the Invoices to open the Invoices list, then select add .

      This opens a blank New Invoice screen where you’ll need to enter the customer details, invoice details, and tax method manually. For more information on adding customer and job details see Adding customer information.

  2. If you created the invoice from a job, select the type of invoice you want to create:

    • New invoice - Create a new invoice from scratch.

    • Convert quote to invoice - Convert an accepted quote into an invoice, see Converting a quote to an invoice.

    A new invoice opens with your customer details already filled in.

Viewing and changing invoice information

Fill in the invoice information sections:

  1. Invoice Details (optional) - Add a brief summary or description for the invoice.

  2. Payment terms - Select the time period for payment. By default, payment is due 14 days after the invoice date.

  3. Tax - Choose whether the prices you enter are Tax Inclusive (GST included) or Tax Exclusive (GST added to the subtotal). For more information, see Tax Inclusive and Exclusive.

  4. Automatic Reminders - Enable or disable payment reminders for your customers. By default, reminders are turned on. For more information, see Automatic Reminders.

Adding items to the invoice

In the Items section of the invoice, add or review all the items that you are invoicing your customer for.

  • If the invoice was created from a converted quote, all the items from that quote, including any discounts, will be filled in automatically.

  • If you’re creating a new invoice from scratch, you’ll need to add items manually.

You can also add extra items to any invoice, even if it was converted from a quote.

To do this, select add in the Item section, then enter the item name or code, quantity, and price, and select Add.

As you add, edit, or remove items, the Subtotal, GST, and Invoice total fields update automatically to reflect your changes.

To remove an item, select it and choose Delete.

Adding Deposits

In the Deposit section, select add to record any deposit payments you’ve already received.

As deposits are added, the Deposits and Balance fields update to show the amount already paid and the remaining balance.

Note:

If the invoice was created by converting a quote with a deposit, this section will automatically include that deposit amount. The Invoice total remains the same — only the balance payable changes.

Payment method options

In the Payment Method section, choose how to handle merchant fees for digital payments:

  • Pass on fee – The fee is charged to your customer (not shown on the invoice but included in their payment receipt).

  • Absorb fee – You cover the merchant fee. The fee isn’t added to the invoice total.

Checking job details

The Associated Job section shows the job number and creation date. Select this row if you want to open and view the job.

Sending the invoice

When your invoice is ready:

  1. Select Send to preview the invoice before sending. You will be shown a preview of the invoice that the customer will receive.

  2. Choose Send via email, or select Save to keep it as a draft.

  3. For more information on sending invoices, see Emailing an invoice or quote to a customer.
